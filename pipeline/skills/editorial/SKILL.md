# Skill: Topic Selection & Editorial Planning

## A topic is a PROMISE, not a subject
Bad: "cat behavior" (subject). Good: "The one thing your cat does that means
'I love you'" (promise). Every topic must state what the viewer GETS.

## Selection filters, in order
1. NICHE FIT — must serve THIS account's audience. A cats account never
   posts AI-tool topics, and vice versa.
2. PROVEN PATTERN — prefer angles that already worked (in lessons/memory or
   competitor trends), with a fresh subject: clone the pattern, never the content.
3. SPECIFICITY — contains a number, a timeframe, a named object, or a
   counter-intuitive claim.
4. EVERGREEN > NEWS unless the trend is under 72h old.

## Diversity rule per batch
No two topics in a batch may share the same angle type. Mix: how-to,
mistake-to-avoid, hidden-reason, cheap-alternative, myth-bust.

## Hard bans
- Income promises, "make $X", get-rich framing (legal rule)
- Anything requiring reposting someone else's footage
- Topics already on the board in the last 20 items (check the recent list)

## Content pillars (balance the calendar)
<!-- pillar framework distilled from coreyhaines31/marketingskills social (MIT) -->
Spread topics across pillars per account, roughly: Educational how-tos 30%,
Industry insight/trends 30%, Myth-busting/contrarian 20%, Story/case 15%,
Promotional 5% max. If the last 5 items lean one pillar, pick from another.
Repurposing mindset: one strong idea = one video + a tweet + a caption angle —
prefer topics rich enough to survive repurposing.

## Topic quality bar
Each topic must name a specific outcome or number ("8-minute", "3 mistakes",
"under $50"). Reject topics that are a category, not a promise.
