# Skill: Short-Form Video Scriptwriting (Reels/Shorts/TikTok)

## The 3-second law
The first beat decides everything. A hook must contain ONE of:
- A specific, surprising claim ("Your cat knows the time you come home — to the minute")
- A pattern interrupt question ("Why does your skin look worse RIGHT after washing?")
- A stakes statement ("This mistake costs new cat owners a vet bill in week one")
NEVER open with greetings, "hey guys", context, or the word "today".

## Structure (6-8 beats, 25-40s total)
1. HOOK (1-2s) — the promise
2. TENSION (2-4s) — why the viewer's current belief/habit is wrong
3-6. PAYOFF beats — one concrete idea per beat, each standing alone
7. TWIST or bonus — the beat people comment about
8. CTA — one action only ("Follow for one tip a day"). Never two CTAs.

## Beat rules
- Voiceover per beat: max 18 words, spoken language, no commas-chains.
- on_screen_text: max 6 words, restates the beat's ONE idea.
- visual_prompt: describe a single concrete shot (subject + action + framing),
  never abstract ("innovation", "success"). Vertical 9:16 composition.
- Numbers beat adjectives: "8-minute workout" > "quick workout".
- Every claim must be true and verifiable. NO income claims, NO medical
  promises, NO "guaranteed". This is a hard legal rule for this company.

## Specificity test (apply to every beat)
If the sentence could appear in ANY video in this niche, rewrite it.
Bad: "Cats are amazing pets." Good: "Cats spend 30% of waking hours grooming — this is why yours over-grooms when stressed."

## Caption + hashtags
Caption: 1 curiosity line + 1 value line. Hashtags: 4-6, niche-specific,
never #fyp #viral #foryou.

## Six hook patterns (rotate; never reuse the same two days in a row)

Structure: hooks are TWO short lines. Line 1 (max ~40 chars) states something
unexpected/specific — never a question. Line 2 contradicts, reframes, or
raises the stakes. Include a digit or metric whenever truthful.
1. NUMBER-LED  — lead with a specific figure. "Cats sleep 16 hours a day. / Yours is faking half of it."
2. CONTRARIAN  — state the common belief, flip it. "Everyone says post daily. / The top 1% post 3x a week."
3. TRANSFORMATION — before vs after with a digit. "30 days ago this page had 0 posts. / Here's what changed."
4. AUTHORITY  — anchor on a known name/tool/brand (truthfully). "NASA studied this for 20 years. / The answer fits in 8 seconds."
5. ADMISSION  — confess a mistake or loss. "We got this wrong for weeks. / Here's the fix."
6. FUTURE SHOCK — a concrete prediction. "This tool won't exist in 2 years. / Learn the skill behind it instead."
Hook families that also work: curiosity ("The real reason X happens isn't what you think"), value ("How to X without Y"), story ("Last week, [unexpected thing] happened").
HARD RULE: no fabricated numbers, names, or claims. If a pattern needs a fact you don't have, pick another pattern.
