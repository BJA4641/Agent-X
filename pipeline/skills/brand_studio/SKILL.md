# Brand Studio — voice & bible playbook

## Voice principles (apply to every brand bible)
1. Clarity over cleverness — if a reader pauses to decode, it failed.
2. Specificity over vagueness — "8-minute morning routine" not "life-changing habits".
3. Customer language over company language — use words the audience actually
   says; mine comments and questions, not marketing decks.
4. Benefits over features — what changes for the viewer, not what the thing is.
5. One idea per piece — every asset makes exactly one promise.

## Building do/don't lists
Do-list items must be concrete behaviors ("open with a number", "name the
tool"), never virtues ("be authentic"). Don't-list must include this
company's hard lines: NO income claims, NO guaranteed results, NO medical
promises, NO fabricated stats/testimonials — regardless of client answers.

## Risk register
Off-limits topics: anything requiring credentials the account doesn't have
(medical/legal/financial advice framed as advice), earnings screenshots,
"passive income" framing, competitor bashing by name.

## Tone spectrum
Define voice on 3 axes: formal↔casual, playful↔serious, bold↔measured —
pick a point on each and give one example sentence per axis. A voice
without example sentences is a wish, not a spec.
